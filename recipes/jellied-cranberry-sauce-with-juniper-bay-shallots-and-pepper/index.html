Jellied Cranberry Sauce with Juniper, Bay, Shallots, and Pepper

Servings: 12 (Makes about 2¾ cups)

Time: 50 minutes, plus 5 hours chilling

Ingredients

  • 1⅓ cups (9⅓ ounces) sugar
  • 2 shallots, chopped fine
  • 8 bay leaves, broken
  • 2 tablespoons juniper berries, lightly crushed
  • 4 cups sweetened cranberry juice
  • 12 ounces (3 cups) fresh or frozen cranberries
  • ¼ teaspoon table salt
  • ¾ teaspoon pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon vanilla extract

Equipment

  • Large Saucepans
  • Fine-Mesh Strainers

Recipe Notes

  1. If you don't wish to unmold the sauce, transfer the mixture to a glass serving bowl in step 3. To control evaporation, use a saucepan with a diameter no greater than 9 inches, and be sure to follow the timings in the recipe. Do not use unsweetened cranberry juice here. 

Instructions

  1. Combine sugar, shallots, bay leaves, and juniper berries in bowl and mash with potato masher until sugar is moist and fragrant. Bring cranberry juice, cranberries, salt, and sugar mixture to boil in large saucepan over medium-high heat, stirring to dissolve sugar. Cook, lowering heat slightly if mixture threatens to boil over, for 10 minutes. Mash cranberries with potato masher. Continue to cook, stirring frequently, until mixture is slightly thickened, 15 minutes longer. (Mixture will be very foamy, but foam will subside as mixture cooks.)
  2. Carefully strain mixture through fine-mesh strainer set over 4-cup liquid measuring cup. Stir and press solids with back of small spoon or ladle until about ¼ cup skins, seeds, and pulp remains in strainer. You should have about 2¾ cups strained mixture (it’s fine if you have a little less; if you have more than 2¾ cups, return strained mixture to saucepan and cook over medium-high heat until reduced to 2¾ cups). Use clean spoon to scrape mixture from bottom of strainer into liquid measuring cup. Discard solids. Add pepper and vanilla and stir until mixture is smooth.
  3. Transfer mixture to 3-cup mold or clean 15-ounce can (put leftover mixture in small mold or bowl). Let cool completely, at least 1 hour. Cover tightly and refrigerate until firm, at least 4 hours or up to 4 days. To unmold, use your fingers to gently pull sauce away from all edges of mold to loosen. Holding plate firmly over mold, invert mold and shake sharply until sauce releases. Remove mold and serve.
