Smoky Broiled Shrimp for Two

Servings: 2

Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces extra-large shrimp (21 to 25 per pound) peeled and deveined, tails left on
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 ½ teaspoons honey
  • ¾ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro
  • Lemon wedges

Equipment

  • Rimmed Baking Sheets
  • Measuring Spoons

Recipe Notes

  1. We prefer untreated shrimp; if yours are already treated with salt or additives such as sodium tripolyphosphate, skip the salting in step 1. This recipe was developed with Diamond Crystal kosher salt. If you’re using Morton, which is denser, use a little less than ¼ teaspoon.

Instructions

  1. Toss shrimp and salt together in bowl; set aside and let sit for 15 to 30 minutes.
  2. Combine butter and honey in small bowl. Cover and microwave until butter is melted, about 40 seconds. Add paprika, cumin, and coriander and stir to combine. Let cool slightly, about 3 minutes. While mixture cools, adjust oven rack 4 inches from broiler element and heat broiler. Line rimmed baking sheet with aluminum foil and set wire rack in sheet.
  3. Spread out shrimp on plate or cutting board and pat dry with paper towels. Return to bowl and pour butter mixture over shrimp. Toss until shrimp are thoroughly and evenly coated, including where they are split from deveining (it’s OK if butter starts to solidify). Arrange shrimp in single layer on prepared rack.
  4. Broil until shrimp are opaque throughout and beginning to lightly char in spots, 3 to 5 minutes. Transfer shrimp to serving platter; sprinkle with cilantro; and serve, passing lemon wedges separately.
